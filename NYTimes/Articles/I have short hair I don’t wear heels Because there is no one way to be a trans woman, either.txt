I walked briskly through Harvard’s campus, but slowed down as I reached the grassy courtyard, where a crowd of mostly gay men and a smattering of women milled about under a giant white tent. It was a drizzly May afternoon in 2017, 20 years since I had graduated, and I was there to attend the college’s queer alumni reception. Yet suddenly, I felt unprepared to see people who had never met me as a woman. I took a breath, then ambled over to the drinks, where I soon spied an old friend named Sean.
