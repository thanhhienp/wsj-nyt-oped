ATLANTA — This, an old saying goes, is “the city too busy to hate,” one of the few places in America where enlightened leaders, black and white, chose prosperity over prejudice and a large black middle class emerged decades ago. Birthplace of Martin Luther King Jr., cradle of the civil rights movement, Atlanta, with its gleaming towers and porch swings, was an American exception. The city managed racial conflict through compromise. It was the black mecca. Or so the story went.
